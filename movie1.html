About Movies

•From 2024 to today, cinemas have been dominated by a mix of animated sensations and blockbuster sequels. In 2024, Inside Out 2 became a global phenomenon, earning $1.7 billion and breaking records as the highest-grossing animated film ever. Marvel’s Deadpool & Wolverine followed closely with $1.33 billion, while family favorites like Despicable Me 4, Moana 2, and Kung Fu Panda 4 pulled huge crowds. Live-action hits such as Dune: Part Two, Wicked, Mufasa: The Lion King, and Godzilla x Kong: The New Empire kept action fans thrilled, while the indie hit The Substance proved small-budget films can still pack a punch. In 2025, the spotlight shifted to Ne Zha 2, which smashed records with over $2 billion, and Disney’s live-action Lilo & Stitch, which crossed the $1 billion mark, showing the continuing global power of beloved stories and animated magic.

How Movie Rentals Are Changing

•The movie rental landscape is rapidly shifting. Traditional physical media has plummeted—U.S. physical product sales (including DVD rentals) fell to around $960 million in 2024, representing just 1.6% of overall home entertainment spending; physical rentals alone dropped over 50% year-over-year Reddit. Major players like Netflix discontinued DVDs, and Redbox—once synonymous with kiosk rentals—entered Chapter 7 bankruptcy, resulting in the shutdown of thousands of kiosks

•Yet, physical media hasn’t completely vanished. In regions like Latin America, the Middle East, and Africa—where internet access may be inconsistent and regional content in demand—DVD rentals via kiosks or local rental shops still persist, serving both cultural and logistical needs LinkedIn. In the UK and other parts of the world, boutique video rental stores survive by diversifying—offering party supplies, private screenings, and immersive experiences—and rely on strong community ties and niche film selections The Guardian. Similarly, newly established stores like Pages & Pixels in Michigan are carving out their place by offering affordable DVDs, books, games, and community activities as an alternative to streaming

•Moreover, there's a shift toward simultaneous ("day-and-date") releases, where films debut on multiple platforms—like theaters and digital rental—on or around the same day. This disrupts traditional windows of exclusivity and caters to evolving consumer expectations for immediate access.
